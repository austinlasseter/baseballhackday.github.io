History

Baseball Hack Day was orginally born out of a Boston area news & geek meetup group Hacks/Hackers and Society of American Baseball Research Boston Chapter after watching terrible collapse of 2011 Red Sox season. Our first event was held at The Boston Globe on March 24, 2012 with 35 participants with 9 projects. In 2013, thoughtbot hosted a 31-participants, 10-projects fun and inspiring hack day in March. In 2014, Baseball Hack day expanded into two cities, Boston and Philadelphia. You can see submissions and winners of the past years: 2013 and 2014. In 2015, we are expanding to Chicago, and San Francisco!

The event is based on the principal of the Hack Day Manifesto. It will always be free to attend, and we are run by volunteers, and we’ll never be for-profit. It's a collective labor of love. If you want to join us in the fun, read this doc, join slack and reach out. Can't wait to hear from you!
